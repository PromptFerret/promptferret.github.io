Noemari comprises those [[Arcanum Mechanica/Races & Species/Overview|Animari]] that possess advanced cognitive abilities, self-awareness, and complex communication skills. Rather than being defined by their physical appearance, this group is united by its mental and cultural attributes.

Under Noemari, sentient life can take many forms—from traditional human shapes to entirely non-humanoid beings, such as those with avian or other exotic morphologies. For instance, a [[Arcanum Mechanica/Races & Species/Animari/Overview|Animari]] that appears predominantly crow-like (retaining most of its natural features) may be considered [[#Aviaryn]] if it demonstrates intelligence and cultural complexity. Likewise, even if a being is primarily human in shape, distinctive crow-like features would still qualify it as [[#Aviaryn]], provided it exhibits advanced cognition and culture.

---
### Mamaryn
>Covers all [mammalian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mammal)-based Noemari, including humans and other pure mammal hybrids.
_Root:_ Derived from the Latin **[mammalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mammal)** (from _mamma_, meaning "breast"), referring to mammals.
### Aviaryn
>Encompasses all bird-based Noemari.
_Root:_ Derived from the Latin **avis** meaning "bird."
### Reptiliryn
>For reptilian-based Noemari.
_Root:_ Derived from the Latin **reptilis** meaning "creeping" or "crawling."
### Amphibaryn
>For amphibian-based Noemari—those with traits of both aquatic and terrestrial life.
_Root:_ Derived from the Greek **amphi** ("both" or "on both sides").
### Aquaryn
>For aquatic, fish-based Noemari.
_Root:_ Derived from the Latin **aqua** meaning "water."
### Insectaryn
>For insect-based Noemari.
_Root:_ Derived from the Latin **insectum**, referring to insects.
### Arachnaryn
>For arachnid-based Noemari, such as spider or scorpion hybrids.
_Root:_ Derived from the Greek **arachne** meaning "spider."
### Crustaryn
>For crustacean-based Noemari, drawing on the forms of crabs, lobsters, and shrimp.
_Root:_ Derived from the Latin **crusta** (or **crustaceus**), referring to crustaceans.
### Cephalopodryn
>For cephalopod-based Noemari, including octopus and squid hybrids.
_Root:_ Derived from the Greek **kephale** ("head") and **podos** ("foot"), which together describe cephalopods.
### Myriapodryn
>For myriapod-based Noemari, such as centipede or millipede-inspired beings.
_Root:_ Derived from the Greek **myrias** meaning "countless," referring to the many-legged creatures.
### Echinodermaryn
For echinoderm-based Noemari, like starfish or sea urchin hybrids.
_Root:_ Derived from the Greek **echinos** ("hedgehog" or "spiny") and **derma** ("skin"), referring to the spiny skin characteristic of echinoderms.
